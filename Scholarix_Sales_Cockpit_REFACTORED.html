01 THE OPENING 0–30 sec · Goal: get 2 minutes
▶ YOU OPEN WITH
"Good morning! Is this Mr. [Name]? Hi Mr. [Name], this is [Your Name] from SGC TECH AI in Dubai. We deliver fixed-price, fixed-timeline Odoo 19 + AI implementations for UAE mid-market businesses. I'll be super direct, just need 2 minutes. Is that okay?"
#01 Objection
"I'm busy / No time / In a meeting"
Your Reply
"I completely understand — that's exactly why I'm calling. Most teams are buried in spreadsheets, WhatsApp threads, and disconnected tools. Just 90 seconds: if I can show you how to recover 20+ hours weekly with Odoo + AI, would that be worth it right now?"
If still resistant — reschedule with specific time: "No problem. When works better — this afternoon at 3 PM, or tomorrow at 10 AM?" Never say "when are you free"
#02 Objection
"Not interested"
Your Reply
"I appreciate your directness. Before I let you go — is it because you're fully satisfied with your current ERP setup, or because you've heard too many generic software pitches?"
If "happy": "If you could fix ONE operations bottleneck immediately, what would it be?" → they reveal pain → "That's exactly where we deploy Odoo + AI."
If "too many pitches": "Fair. We're not selling licenses only. We deliver implementation, compliance, integrations, and training under one scope." → pivot to value.
#03 Objection
"Remove me from your list / Don't call again"
Your Reply
"Absolutely. I apologize for the interruption. Before I remove you — is it because this isn't relevant to your business, or just bad timing right now?"
"Not relevant": "Understood. I'll remove you immediately. Have a great day." Mark as Do Not Call
"Bad timing": "Should I follow up in 3 months, or never contact you again?" → schedule or close cleanly.
02 AUTHORITY & DISCOVERY 1–3 min · Qualify & uncover pain
Superpower: Qualify authority BEFORE investing time. One question doubles your conversion rate.
▶ AUTHORITY QUESTION
"Before we go further — for an Odoo + AI implementation, are you the decision-maker, or should we include a partner, finance lead, or operations head from the start?"
#04 Objection
"I need to check with my partner / boss / finance team"
Your Reply
"That makes sense. From experience — when the decision-maker isn't in the initial call, we end up repeating everything twice and important details get lost. What makes more sense: can you bring [decision-maker] in now, or should I call back when you can both join for 30 minutes?"
Golden rule: If you can't get the decision-maker on 2 attempts, don't book the meeting. Mark for 1-month follow-up.
#05 Objection
"Just send me information first"
Your Reply
"Every business is unique — generic brochures won't map your real workflow. The scoping call is more valuable: we map your current process and show a tailored Odoo 19 + AI architecture. 30 minutes, and if after 10 you feel it's not valuable, you can end the call. Fair? Thursday 11 AM or Friday 3 PM?"
If they still insist: Send info BUT lock a tentative meeting on the same call. "If it looks good, can we discuss Tuesday at 2 PM?" Send info + calendar invite immediately.
▶ DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (in order)
1. "Walk me through the full flow — lead/order to invoice/collection. Where are Excel and WhatsApp still involved?"
2. "How many hours per day are lost in admin, reconciliation, or manual reporting?"
3. "Which teams are impacted most — sales, finance, operations, or projects?"
4. "Are VAT/Corporate Tax/RERA/PDPL requirements handled in-system or manually?"
→ CALCULATE COST OUT LOUD
[Example] 6 hrs/day × 3 people = 18 hrs daily
18 × 22 days = 396 hrs/month
396 × AED 100/hr = AED 39,600/month
× 12 = AED 475,200/year LOST to manual processes
#06 Objection
"We're already using [existing system / software]"
Your Reply
"That's great — it means you value technology. Are you 100% happy, or are there still gaps handled manually? Most ERP setups solve 70–80% and leave integration, reporting, and adoption gaps. We either optimize your current stack or migrate in phases with minimal disruption. Worth 20 minutes to explore?"
If they claim 100% automated: Drill: "Automatic invoice from orders? Real-time inventory across all locations? Auto reports without manual compilation?" → they'll admit gaps.
#07 Objection
"We're too small for this"
Your Reply
"Actually — that's perfect. Mid-market teams benefit fastest because every recovered hour directly improves cashflow and execution. Our phased delivery model is designed for lean teams, with training and change management built in. Want to see how?"
03 SOFT PITCH & MEETING LOCK 1–2 min · Create curiosity
Critical rule: Do NOT explain everything on the phone. Create enough curiosity to get the meeting. Show, don't tell.
▶ THE SOFT PITCH (90 seconds max)
"Based on what you shared — high manual workload, reporting delays, and compliance pressure — here is what we do: Odoo 19 architecture + AI copilots + UAE compliance-first configuration. We connect finance, sales, operations, and reporting in one operating system, with integrations and team training included. I'm not going to over-explain on this call; it's easier to SHOW you your exact future-state flow. I'd like to offer a free 30-minute scoping consultation to map your process, identify quick wins, and estimate year-1 ROI. Open to that?"
#08 Objection
"We don't have budget right now"
Your Reply
"This scoping call is completely free, and you should still see the numbers even before budget approval. If inefficiency is costing AED 475,200/year and implementation is AED 72K/year, you're protecting margin, not creating expense. We also structure delivery in fixed milestones so there are no open-ended retainers. Give me 30 minutes, then you decide. Fair?"
If genuinely cash-constrained: "We can phase modules and timelines so value lands early. Let me first confirm if this is commercially viable for your business."
#09 Objection
"We need to think about it" (early-stage)
Your Reply
"Of course! But — I'm not asking you to commit to anything. I'm asking for 30 minutes to SHOW you something. Like a car showroom — you're not buying by looking, you're exploring. After the meeting you can think all you want. But at least you'll have complete information to think about, instead of guessing in the dark. Thursday 11 AM or Friday 2 PM — which is less busy?"
#10 Objection
"We tried automation before and it didn't work"
Your Reply
"I'm sorry that happened — that's frustrating. Can you tell me what failed: scope, timeline, adoption, or support? [LISTEN] That's exactly where we differ: fixed scope, fixed timeline, operator-led rollout, compliance built in from day one, and structured training/change management. Worth 30 minutes to compare approaches? If you see the same red flags, we stop there. You're fully in control."
Insight: This is a GOOD sign — they believe in automation, they just had a bad vendor. Position yourself as the fix.
#11 Objection
"Can we do this next month instead?"
Your Reply
"We cap onboarding capacity to protect delivery quality. Scoping this week means earlier go-live and earlier savings. Delaying the discovery simply extends current inefficiency and compliance risk. Can we at least lock the scoping call this week? It doesn't commit you."
▶ MEETING LOCK SCRIPT
"Perfect! Thursday at 11 AM UAE time. 30 minutes on Zoom. Email for the invite? WhatsApp same as this number? Great. You'll receive: calendar invite, WhatsApp confirmation, agenda, and required prep list. Please include operations + finance so we can validate process, compliance, and ROI in one call. If anything changes, message me and we'll reschedule properly. Fair?"
⚡ Send calendar invite + WhatsApp confirmation within 5 minutes of hanging up. Add to CRM. Set day-before reminder.
04 DEMO & CLOSE 30 min meeting · Goal: close TODAY
Meeting structure: Business context (3 min) → Process map (5 min) → Odoo 19 + AI walkthrough (12 min) → ROI & compliance fit (4 min) → Readiness (2 min) → Decision path (2 min) → Next steps (2 min)
▶ READINESS CHECK (Min 26–28)
"On a scale of 1 to 10, where 10 means ready to move forward today and 1 means this isn't right at all — where are you right now?"
8–10 → "What would make this a 10? What's holding you back?" → address → close.
5–7 → "What would make this a 10?" → address real concern → re-ask scale.
1–4 → "Not the right fit right now. What would need to change?" → qualify out cleanly.
▶ THE AAU CLOSE (Assumptive · Alternative · Urgency)
"Based on everything: clear inefficiency cost, strong year-1 ROI, and a clear fit for your operations. I don't see a reason to delay. Let's start with the agreed fixed-scope implementation plan. We can structure this as Starter, Growth, or Enterprise based on your complexity. To proceed, I need two items: your legal entity details and your package decision. Which option fits [Company] best today?"
🤐 SILENCE RULE: After asking for the order — STOP TALKING. Count to 30. First to speak loses.
#12 Objection
"It's too expensive"
Your Reply
"If I can show verified savings and faster execution, would this still feel expensive? [WAIT] This is a margin-protection decision, not a software expense. The real comparison is implementation cost versus ongoing monthly leakage from manual operations. Should we proceed with the right-fit package for your team?"
Reframe: "Compare unit economics before and after rollout by process cycle, not just subscription line items."
#13 Objection
"Can you give us a discount?"
Your Reply
"We don't compete on random discounting because delivery quality drops when scope is underpriced. What we can do is optimize commercial structure: phase rollout, milestone billing, or value-add items like extra training and reporting packs. Would you like the best-fit commercial structure or the lowest sticker price?"
If they insist on price reduction: "What commercial constraint do we need to solve: budget timing, scope size, or payment terms?" → adjust structure, not delivery quality. Never discount >15% without commitment trade
#14 Objection
"I need to discuss with my partner first"
Your Reply
"Absolutely. Let's schedule a 15-min call with you AND your partner tomorrow at 11 AM — I'll address questions directly. Usually gets approval in 24 hours vs. weeks of back-and-forth. I'll prepare a one-page business case for your partner: problem, solution, ROI, timeline, risk mitigation. Should I prepare the contract too so we can move quickly if you both agree?"
Never let them discuss alone — you can't address objections you don't hear. Always get on the partner call.
#15 Objection
"We need to think about it" (at closing stage)
Your Reply
"What specifically should we de-risk before you decide? Delivery confidence, ROI assumptions, compliance fit, integration risk, or team adoption? If you name the concern, we can solve it now with a concrete plan. [LISTEN] Every delay extends current inefficiency cost. Shall we move forward with the agreed scope and timeline?"
#16 Objection
"This looks complicated for my team"
Your Reply
"For your team, daily usage becomes simpler than today. Complexity is handled in architecture, not dumped on end users. We include role-based screens, SOP-driven training, and go-live support so adoption is structured, not left to chance."
#17 Objection
"The ROI sounds too good to be true"
Your Reply
"Fair point. We keep ROI claims auditable. We baseline your current cycle times, error rates, and labor load, then measure post-go-live deltas. This is not hype; it's operator-grade measurement. If you want, we'll walk you through the exact assumptions line by line."

05 POST-CLOSE & GHOSTING After yes · Prevent buyer's remorse
▶ CONTRACT CAPTURE
"Excellent decision! Let me capture the implementation brief: 1) Legal entity details, 2) Business license, 3) Billing details, 4) Project owner + approver, 5) Technical contacts, 6) Target go-live window. [Confirm back] We'll send the scoped proposal, timeline, and milestone plan within 2 hours."
⚡ Within 1 hour: WhatsApp recap + scoped proposal + implementation timeline + stakeholder invite + CRM stage updated
#18 Situation
They didn't show up for the booked meeting
Your Action
Call within 5 minutes of meeting time: "Hi Mr. [Name], we had a scoping call at 11 AM. Is everything okay?" [Listen] "No problem, these things happen. I have your process map template ready — can we reschedule tomorrow same time, or this afternoon at 3 PM?"
If they no-show twice: Send WhatsApp: "We've had to reschedule twice. Should I close your file and follow up in 3 months, or schedule one final meeting with a firm commitment?" After 2 no-shows — stop chasing. Mark 3-month follow-up.
#19 Situation
They said yes but went silent after contract sent
Your Action
Day 3: WhatsApp gentle check-in. Day 5: Call — "Did you receive the proposal and timeline? Any blocker on scope or approvals?" Create urgency: "To secure your implementation slot, we need confirmation this week." Day 7: Send break-up email.
Break-up email: "Should I close your file? I've reached out several times since our meeting. A simple 'not interested' is fine. If you're still interested, reply 'INTERESTED' and tell me what's holding you back. Please reply by Friday or I'll close your file."
#20 Situation
Buyer's remorse — they want to cancel after signing
Your Action
"I understand. Help me understand what changed: scope, team readiness, budget timing, or risk concerns? [LISTEN] Most remorse comes from uncertainty, not mismatch. Let me address the exact concern with a revised execution plan and risk controls. If it still does not fit, we'll close respectfully. Would you allow one alignment call before canceling?"
06 CULTURAL QUICK-SWITCH Adapt tone by client background
CX1 Arab / Emirati
Relationship first · respect & formality · religious references welcome · decisions take longer · face-to-face preferred
Tone Adjustments
Open: "Assalamu alaikum, Mr. Abdullah. How are you today, Sir?" — take time for pleasantries.
Close: "Building a relationship with you matters — not just a sale. This will serve you well, Inshallah. Shall we move forward together as partners?"
Never: Pressure-close · "limited time" framing · skip pleasantries · call on Fridays
Always: Use "Sir" · be patient · offer face-to-face · reference Arab businesses you've helped
CX2 Indian / Pakistani
Detail-driven · ROI obsessed · negotiation expected · multiple decision-makers · thoroughness over speed
Tone Adjustments
Open: "Good morning, Mr. Patel. I'll be direct — we deliver Odoo 19 + AI implementations with measurable ROI and fixed timelines. May I take 2 minutes?"
ROI emphasis: show every number, every step, on screen. "Investment AED 72K/yr → Savings AED 258K/yr → Net profit AED 186K → 258% ROI → 2.8-month payback."
Expect negotiation: never give best price first — leave room.
Never: Take offense at price objections · skip technical details · rush decision
Always: Detailed ROI · written documentation · persistent follow-up · prepare for partner involvement
CX3 Western Expat
Direct · efficient · time-conscious · ROI-driven · clear timelines · less negotiation, more decision
Tone Adjustments
Open: "Hi David, this is [Name] from SGC TECH AI. I'll keep this brief. We deploy Odoo + AI systems that replace manual workflows and fragmented tools. You're likely losing ~AED 25K/month to inefficiency. Worth a 30-min meeting?"
Close: "Current loss AED 27,500/mo · our cost AED 5,999/mo · payback 3 months · 258% ROI. I need your decision by Friday for December start. Yes or no?"
Never: Excessive small talk · vague pricing · miss deadlines · over-explain
Always: Be punctual · efficient · ROI-focused · structured · clear deliverables
